Why Am I Still in a Brace or on Crutches?

The – ouch...uh oh... What was that? The sound of ball and bat connecting... The contact of racket and a birdie... Take your pick. Maybe it was the unwanted sound of a ligament popping. Maybe it was (I hope that wasn’t me) bone cracking. Maybe it was the groan of a baby boomer who realizes that he or she has just pushed his/her body further than it wanted or was willing to go.

Baby Boomers often have a different view of aging: they seem to have a bit of denial and think it doesn’t happen to them. Their bodies are letting them in on the “News Flash...” Just visit any Emergency Room and you will find that the waiting room isn’t filled just with kids who fell off their skateboards anymore. And when older people get hurt, they may find that it can take significantly longer for their injuries to heal.

The Pathway to Health Healing Continued

A weakening immune system as people age may also play a role in injury recovery, as may a decrease in blood flow and changes in the wound-healing your body undertakes that is set off by injury. There is also the issue of the decreased bone and muscle mass that often comes with aging.

The Baby Boom generation was generally less physically active than their elders had been both in the house and at work. Poor eating habits and sitting in a chair all the time have actually brought about lots of cases of diabetes, weight problems, high cholesterol, and high blood pressure amongst baby boomers in retirement.

A way baby boomers could improve health and extend the aging procedure is through natural techniques and solutions for healing. The “ancients” typically relied on natural treatments for recovering because they didn’t have a doctor’s office or local healthcare facility nearby. Today, many people rely on drugs, which only deal with the symptoms, not the trouble.
